PhD Thesis

(Pursuing since 2011)

My research topic is in the broad area of wireless networks (WNs), specifically wireless local area networks (WLANs) and underwater sensor networks (UWSNs). In my PhD thesis, I have attempted to explore some previously unaddressed issues on collision in WNs. In particular, I have considered both the unintensional and intensional collisions. Addressing the unintensional collision in distributed control of shared channel access is challenging due to the lack of coordination in data transmission attempts generated from the users. On the other hand, intensional collision, i.e., jamming, in WNs highly degrades the network performance. Detection and prevention of different kind of jamming are also challenging.

When I started with my research back in 2011, I was quick to get familiar with the wireless networks and the network simulator NS-2. However, I soon realized another advanced network simulator NS-3. Therefore, I have shifted smoothly to the NS-3 for the remaining works of my research problems.
